Introduction to Astronomy

Astronomy is a section of science that examines celestial objects such as galaxies, planets, stars, comets, meteors, and other things in space.
It is originated from the Greek word “astro,” which implies to “star,” and “nomos,” which denotes to “law”.
As to combine this two words, it will form a definition known as “star law”. Astrology is not the same as astronomy. Don't be bewildered by this two.
Astrology has no relationship to astronomy and not considered in science.

The field of astronomy is also defined by what the person sees in the sky and even instruments used far away in space like telescopes.
Historically, in the early period, the only used to observe astronomical objects is by the use of naked eye.
This is due to the fact that there is no any instrument during the old era.

History of Universe

Theories of the Universe

There are vast theories about the universe and some of those are the following:



CREATIONISM THEORY

“All matter in the universe were created in a span of six days.”
The monotheistic religions of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam all believe that the cosmos was created by a superior being.

HINDU BELIEFS

“The universe is oscillating which expands out and will collapse eventually.”
The Hindu text Rigveda describes the universe as an oscillating universe in which a "cosmic egg" named Brahmanda emerges from a single concentrated point called Bindu and afterwards collapses.

AFRICAN BELIEFS

"All the contents of the universe were vomitted by a god."
In the african belief, the Kuba people of Central Africa relate the account of the creator god Mbombo (or Bumba),
who, alone on a dark and water-covered Earth, suffered an acute stomach discomfort and vomited the stars, sun, and moon.
Moreover, man and some of the animals included in what Mbombo vomited.

STEADY STATE THEORY

“The universe expands but its density does not change.”
Fred Hoyle, Thomas Gold and Hermann Bondi (1948)
As the name implies, the word steady state denotes as stable or firm that does not alters over the years.
The steady-state theory has no origin or end in time where galaxies of various ages are mixed together.
This theory was not scientific at all, but purely philosophical.

BIG BANG THEORY

Stages of Big Bang Theory:

The Big Bang
(0 to 10^-43 sec)
On reviewing the universe origin, the Big Bang Theory hypothesizes that the universe is 13.8 billion years ago,
where expansion from a tiny, dense and hot mass provides its present size and causes a much cooler state.



Inflation Theory (10^-32 sec to 10 sec)
The Inflation Theory is a stage where the formation of quarks, hadrons, and leptons occurs.



Post Inflation (20 mins)
Afterward, nucleosynthesis arises. This is where the production of protons, neutrons, atomic nuclei, hydrogen, helium, and lithium happenned in just 20 minutes.



Condensation Process (380,000 to 100 mil years)
In this stage, the cooling process takes place where cosmic microwave background radiation is observed. By means of nucleosynthesis in stars, carbon and other components heavier than carbon were also produced.



Clustering of Galaxies (9.8 billion years to present)
Finally, at about 9.8 billion, the objects related to space like the solar system was formed which is after the Big Bang. This stage where the solar system emerged.

National Aeronautics and Space Administration or NASA is a U.S. government agency responsible for science and technology related to air and space.

During Oct. 1, 1958. the agency was formed to oversee U.S. space exploration and aeronautics research.
